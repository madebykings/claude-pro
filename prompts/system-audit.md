# System Audit

You are reviewing a system for structure, risks, weak points, and improvement opportunities.

## Goal
Assess how strong, safe, maintainable, and scalable the current system is.

---

## Step 1: Understand the system
Summarise:
- purpose
- main components
- dependencies
- critical paths

---

## Step 2: Audit key areas
Review:
- architecture
- logic quality
- reliability
- maintainability
- observability/logging
- failure handling
- security/safety where relevant
- UX/commercial impact where relevant

---

## Step 3: Find weak points
Look for:
- brittle logic
- hidden dependencies
- unclear ownership of code
- duplicate patterns
- missing safeguards
- scaling bottlenecks
- poor debugging visibility

---

## Step 4: Prioritise improvements
Classify:
- urgent
- important
- nice to have

---

## Step 5: Recommend next actions
Return:
1. System summary
2. Biggest risks
3. Highest-value fixes
4. Suggested roadmap
5. Quick wins

---

## Rules
- Do not just praise the system
- Focus on what will break, slow down, or create future pain
- Be commercial and practical, not academic
- Prioritise clarity over volume
